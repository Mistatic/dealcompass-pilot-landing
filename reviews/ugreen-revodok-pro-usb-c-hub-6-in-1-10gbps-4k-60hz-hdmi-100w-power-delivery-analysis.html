Who This Is Best For

  • USB-C laptop users who need one-cable HDMI, charging, and accessory connectivity.
  • Remote workers running one external 4K monitor plus keyboard/mouse and one extra USB device.
  • Traveling professionals who want a portable hub instead of a full desktop dock.

3 Things We Like

  • Strong day-to-day spec mix: 10Gbps transfer support, 4K 60Hz HDMI, and up to 100W PD input.
  • Helps simplify desk setup and cut cable swapping during laptop dock/undock cycles.
  • No extra software needed for standard data/video/power workflows.

Tradeoffs to Know

  • Six ports can be limiting if you need Ethernet, SD cards, or dual-display support.
  • Actual charging delivered to your laptop is typically lower than the charger's rated input because of hub overhead.
  • 4K 60Hz success still depends on your laptop USB-C video capabilities and cable quality.

Buying Heuristic

If your setup is one external monitor, charging, and 2–3 peripherals, this is a practical fit. If you already expect dual displays or 5+ connected accessories, move directly to a larger dock.

FAQ

  • Will this hub always output 4K at 60Hz?
    It can when your laptop supports the required USB-C video mode and your cable/monitor chain also supports 4K 60Hz.
  • Does 100W PD mean the laptop receives 100W?
    Not necessarily. 100W is usually input passthrough capacity; usable output to the laptop is lower.
  • Is this enough for a permanent desk setup?
    Yes for light-to-moderate setups. For heavier workflows, a full dock usually scales better long term.
